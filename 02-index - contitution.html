The Youth For Christ Fellowship

2025 Written Constitution of The Youth For Christ Fellowship
Ganaja NewLayout Lokoja, Kogi State. Nigeria.


Aims and Objectives

The aims and objectives of the fellowship are as follows: To encourage members to be dedicated to the service of our Lord Jesus Christ. To promote Christianity, unity and spirituality among members. To give every member an equal opportunity to fully participate in the activities of the fellowship. To encourage the participation of members in the affairs,programmes and activities of the Church. To enhance interaction, cordial relationship and objectiveness among members.


Membership

Membership into the youth fellowship is opened to every youth member of the church from the age of 12years and above and who believe in our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ and also in the doctrines and practices of the church.


Registration and Monthly Dues

Registration; the annual/yearly registration for membership into the fellowship is categorized into the following:

  • Executive Members ₦500
  • Working Class Members ₦500
  • Student Members ₦300

    • Monthly Dues

    The monthly dues of the fellowship are also categorized into the following:

  • Executive Members ₦300
  • Working Class Members ₦200
  • Student Members ₦100

    • Any member who defaults in their financial obligation to the fellowship for the period of three months shall be liable for violating the rules of the fellowship.


    Fellowship Executives

    The Executive offices of the fellowship are comprises of the following:

  • The President
  • The Vice President
  • The General Secretary
  • The Assistant General Secretary
  • The Treasurer
  • The Financial Secretary
  • The Public Relations Officer 1&2
  • The Drama Coordinator
  • The Music Director
  • The Bible Study Coordinator
  • The Prayer Coordinator
  • The Welfare Director
  • The Media Director


    • Functions of the Various Officers


    The President

  • Shall preside over the general affairs of the fellowship.
  • Shall summon executive meeting in conjunction with the general secretary.
  • Shall authenticate and authorize expenditure and take decisions on the fellowship’s behalf.
  • Shall be the signatory A’ to the fellowship bank account.
  • Shall ensure that the regular fellowship days are observed.
  • Shall consult and liaise with fellowship coordinators from time to time for best result.
  • Shall ensure that the fellowship operates in line with doctrine and practices of the united evangelical church.
  • Shall be answerable to the authority of the church.
  • Shall supervise the activities of the various units and organs of the fellowship.

    • The Vice President

  • Shall perform the duty of the president in the presidents’ absence.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or the executive council of the fellowship.

    • The General Secretary

  • Shall carry out ALL secretariat duties and correspondences of the fellowship.
  • Shall prepare the agenda for meetings of the fellowship. Shall be the signatory B’ to the fellowship bank account.
  • Shall be in charge and/or the custodian of the properties of the fellowship.
  • Shall send notices of Executives and/or General meetings as directed by the President.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/ or the executive council.

    • The Assistant General Secretary

  • Shall from time to time assist the General Secretary in performing duties.
  • Shall perform the duties of the General Secretary upon the absence of the General Secretary.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or the executive council.

    • The Treasurer

  • Shall collect monies from the financial secretary within 12 hours of collection.
  • Shall keep the monies of the fellowship.
  • Shall keep an appropriate record of income and expenditure.
  • Shall be the signatory C’ to the fellowship account.
  • Shall keep an imprest as agreed by the Executives for the purpose of emergencies.
  • Shall only release monies that are approved by the president and/or executive council.
  • Shall liaise with the financial secretary and prepare a financial report when required by the fellowship.
  • Shall be the custodian of the account cheque booklet.
  • Shall submit the financial book(s) for auditing when required by the fellowship.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or the executive council.

    • The Financial Secretary

  • Shall collect ALL monies as deem fit by the fellowship.
  • Shall keep an up to date financial records of income and expenditure of the fellowship.
  • Shall maintain the accounting book(s) and submit same for auditing when required by the fellowship.
  • Shall liaise with the treasurer and prepare a financial report when required by the fellowship.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned by the president and/or executive council.

    • The Public Relations Officer 1&2

  • Shall be responsible for press and the publicity affairs of the fellowship.
  • Shall be the image maker of the fellowship.
  • Shall pass information and notices of fellowships and meetings to members.
  • Shall serve as liaison officer between the fellowship and the general public.
  • Shall maintain good and cordial relationship with other fellowships and organs of the church on the fellowship’s behalf.

    • The Drama Coordinator

  • Shall oversee drama activities in the fellowship.
  • Shall assist to coordinate drama for the church when required.
  • Shall be the head of the drama unit of the fellowship.
  • Shall work hand in hand with general secretary of the fellowship.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or the executive council.

    • The Music Director

  • Shall be in-charge of the music department and its activities in the fellowship.
  • Shall work hand in hand with the Temple Choir Master.
  • Shall be the head of the youth praise band.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.

    • The Bible Study Coordinator

  • Shall prepare Bible study guide for the fellowship.
  • Shall coordinate Bible study programmes for the fellowship.
  • Shall work hand in hand with the Sunday School coordinator of the Church.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned by the president and/or executive council.

    • The Prayer Coordinator

  • Shall organize prayer programme for the fellowship.
  • Shall from time to time work with the prayer band of the church.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or executive council.

    • The Welfare Director

  • Shall be in-charge of the welfare of ALL registered members.
  • Shall inform the fellowship of its members who may be getting married or had a child birth or sick or involved in an accident or having a ceremony.
  • Shall coordinate the activities of love feast/get-to-gather.
  • Shall ensure even distribution of food and drinks at ceremonies.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or executive council.

    • The Decoration Coordinator

  • Shall carry out the decoration of the church Alter upon the approval of the Local Church Committee.
  • Shall coordinate the decoration of the church auditorium as deem fit.
  • Shall be the head of the decoration unit.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or the executive council.

    • The Media Director

  • Shall direct and produce the programme and activities of the church and youth fellowship in ‘still and motion’ pictures.
  • Shall be in-charge of the media equipment of the fellowship.
  • Shall work hand in hand with the general secretary of the church.
  • Shall be answerable to the fellowship president.
  • Shall perform any other duty as assigned to by the president and/or executive council.

    • Election

    Eligibility

    Election and appointment into offices is opened to All registered, dedicated, committed and financially up-to-date members of the fellowship. Such a member MUST also; Be a member of the church for over six months. Be punctual to church meetings and attends fellowship regularly. Be ready to serve and lead in humility and character. Be a baptized member and of a good standing. The post of vice president, assistant secretary and Ass music director is reserved for members in secondary school atmost SSS1.


    Tenure of Office

    An elected executive officer shall hold a particular office for a period of three (3) years and shall be eligible for re-election for a second and final three (3) year term, if found competent. Same goes for an appointed executive officer.


    In a case where vacancies exist in any office(s) for any reason, the executives of the fellowship shall deliberate and conduct a bye election or selection as the case maybe for an eligible person(s) to occupy the vacant office(s). For office(s) where there are vice or assistants, such shall take over pending when the executives takes a final decision.


    Resignation

    In such a case where an elected/selected exco member willingly wishes to resign, such a person shall write a letter of resignation to the fellowship Exco, the fellowship exco will in turn inform the Church and the Superintendency Exco.


    Mode of Election

    The election shall be by secret ballot and to be presided over by The Superintendency Youth Fellowship Exco, The Fellowship Coordinator(s) or any other Ordained Elder, Deacons/Deaconesses as appointed by the Local Church Committee for the exercise. Electoral guidelines will be read to the electorates by the presiding officer.

    Electorates shall write the names of their choice candidates on the ballot paper and drop in the ballot boxes. At the end of vote casts, the presiding officer shall analyze and collate the vote casts.

    Candidates who scored the highest number of votes shall be declared winner upon meeting up with the electoral requirement.


    Organs of the Fellowship

    The fellowship shall be made up of the following organs;

  • Drama unit: this shall comprise of registered and interested members of the fellowship. Interested members of the church can also become members being invited to perform in plays and drama.
  • Youth Praise Band: this shall also comprise of registered members of the fellowship. This unit in other words, is the youth choir. There activities shall be subject to the supervision of the fellowship president.
  • Decoration unit: this shall comprise of all creative registered members of the fellowship.
  • Media unit: this shall comprise of registered members of the fellowship who has knowledge in mass media, graphics design and computer.

    • The various organs of the fellowship and their heads shall be answerable to the fellowship executive members and the fellowship president shall supervise their activities.


    Meeting Days

  • The fellowship shall hold its general meeting every Sunday between the hours of 4:00 to 6:00pm
  • The fellowship president shall from to time to time call for an executive council meeting.
  • The fellowship shall not hold its meeting on every last Sunday of the month; this is based on church arrangement for zonal/house caring fellowship.

    • An announcement will be made by the church general secretary or service moderator whether or not if the fellowship will hold on a particular Sunday.


    Source of Income

    The source of income of the fellowship shall include:

  • Members’ registration
  • Offering during Fellowship meetings
  • Mini launching
  • Tithe (as a member so wish)
  • Investment/Business
  • Freewill donation
  • Fine

    • Any other accepted source(s) of income by the Fellowship

    Bank Account

  • Pending the successful opening of fellowship cooperate account; All income should be banked with the church account on a monthly basis by the treasurer and incase of withdrawal , the president, treasurer and secretary or any of the above two can sign.
  • The Fellowship shall operate a current account with any new generation bank of her choice. (Fellowship Acc. Details)
    • The President, General Secretary and Treasurer shall be signatories to the account.
  • The President and any two of the signatories can sign for withdrawal on behalf of the Fellowship.
  • The President shall be signatory A’ the General Secretary shall be signatory B’ while the Treasurer shall be signatory C’.

    • Financial Benefits/Welfare

  • The Fellowship reserves the right to support a bonafide member as itemized in a – d bellow. Such a member must have been a registered and financial member of the Fellowship for not less than six (6) months in all cases.
  • Any registered and financial member who meets up 100% attendance will get his or her full benefit package.
  • Any registered and financial member, who fails to meet up with 100% attendance, will only get 50% benefit package.
  • In the case of a member in the Diaspora, the fellowship exco will meet and discuss as appropriate.
  • In a case where two qualified members are siblings or a couple, the fellowship Exco shall combine their benefit and it will be reduced by a certain percentage.
  • In a case where more than two siblings are qualified for the benefit, the fellowship Exco shall meet and decide as appropriate. However, the reduction in percentage will be applied. (Subject to change as prizes of commodities demands)

    • Marriage

  • Any member of the Fellowship who is getting married must: Notify the Exco of the Fellowship one (1) month to the due date of the wedding with the wedding card. If a member fails to notify the Exco as specified, he/she stands to forfeit the benefits thereof.
  • The Fellowship will present a souvenir worth Seven Thousand Naira, only (N 7,000.00)
  • The Fellowship will attend the wedding in their numbers, if the wedding is holding in Lokoja and refreshment should be adequately provided by the celebrant.
  • In a case where the wedding is holding outside Lokoja, the exco will meet and resolve as appropriate.
  • There shall be a formal invitation to the instrumentalist/ music group (Youth Praise Band) and refreshment (crate of soft drink and food) will be provided by the celebrant.

    • Naming Ceremony/Dedication

  • A Fellowship member that has child’s naming ceremony or dedication thanksgiving will notify the Fellowship a week before the due date.
  • The Fellowship shall support the member with the sum of Three Thousand Naira (N 3,000.00) and members shall attend in their numbers. The Three Thousand Naira (₦3,000) benefit can only be enjoyed for the child’s naming ceremony or dedication thanksgiving and not on the two occasions.
  • In a case where the member decides not to organize naming ceremony or child dedication, the said amount will be presented to such a member as a gift to the child.

    • Sickness/Accident

  • On the ground that the Fellowship is aware that such a member is sick or involved in an accident and hospitalized, the Fellowship will send delegates to visit, pray and support the member with the sum of Two Thousand Naira (N2,000.00) only.

    • Death

    The fellowship members look forward to the second coming of our master and king, Jesus Christ but in the case were death comes earlier, the Fellowship exco shall deliberate and undertake as follow:

  • Where the Fellowship losses a member, a condolence visit will be paid to the family and a post humors donation of N 5,000.00 will be made to the next of kin of the late member within 7 days of the incidence.
  • Where a member losses a biological family member (father, mother, child, brother or sister), the Fellowship will pay a condolence visit and donate the sum of N2, 000.00 within 7 days of the incidence.

    • Note: In all cases, convocation of the fellowship shall pay a condolence visit at the residence of the deceased member and or relation as the case may be.


    Impeachment.

    In a situation in which an exco member is not committed in discharging his/her duties effectively, such a person is liable to be impeached by a 2/3 majority of the general congress.


    Discipline

    Any member or group of members involved in any act of misconduct, i.e fighting, mischief, quarreling, embezzlement of fellowship funds, immorality or any other behavior that is tantamount to jeopardizing the good image of the fellowship shall be disciplined as deemed fit by the exco.
    In a case where such a member is an Exco, he or she will be suspended from office and will not be in a decision making position pending when a constituted disciplinary committee completes their assignment.


    Amendment/ Review of the constitution

    This constitution may be amended in a general meeting upon a motion moved to that effect by a bonafide member, seconded and supported by a 2/3 majority of the registered members present. The constitution may be reviewed from time to time as existence dictate.


    Supremacy of the Constitution

    The provision of this constitution shall be supreme and shall have binding force on all the organs and all registered members of the youth fellowship and it shall take its effect from the date of adoption of the constitution in the Youth General Meeting (YGM) and dully endorsed by the following trustees:
